💡 Smart advice

Tips every woman traveller needs to know

Tried, tested, and shared by thousands of women in our community. Because good advice should be free.

All travel guides →
01

Always share your itinerary with someone back home

Even a quick message with hotel names, flight numbers and your check-in days can be a lifesaver. Use an app like Google Maps to share your live location with a trusted friend.

02

Book the first night's accommodation in advance

Arriving in a new city tired and without a plan is a recipe for stress. Having just the first night sorted gives you a safe base to work from — improvise from there.

03

Learn 5 words in the local language

Hello, thank you, excuse me, help, and the name of your accommodation. That's it — locals instantly warm to you and it can get you out of tricky situations.

04

Trust your gut — always

You have a built-in safety system. If a situation, person, or neighbourhood feels off, move on without guilt. No experience is worth ignoring your instincts for.

05

Keep digital and physical copies of documents

Passport, insurance, visa — email them to yourself, save them offline, and keep a photo on your phone. Losing your passport is stressful enough without the panic of having no backup.

💬 Questions

Things our readers ask all the time

Answers from real women in our community — no generic advice, just honest experience.

Ask in the community →
Is it safe to travel solo as a woman? ▼
Yes — millions of women travel solo every year and have incredible, safe experiences. The key is preparation: research your destination, share your itinerary, trust your instincts, and connect with communities like ours where women share real on-the-ground safety info. Is there risk? Like any travel, yes. But informed solo travel is genuinely one of the most empowering things you can do.
What are the best solo destinations for first-time solo female travellers? ▼
For a first solo trip, our community consistently recommends: Japan (incredibly safe, easy to navigate), Portugal (welcoming, affordable, beautiful), New Zealand (stunning nature, very solo-friendly), Iceland (peaceful and well-touristed), and Thailand (well-worn solo female trail with great infrastructure). All have strong communities of solo female travellers to connect with.
How do I meet other women travellers on the road? ▼
Stay in hostels (even booking a private room gives you access to common spaces), join our SweetTravel travel buddy finder, use Facebook groups for your destination, book group tours for a day or two, and frequent the same café more than once — regularity builds friendships faster than anything. Our community forum is also a brilliant place to find women heading to the same destination.
What travel insurance should I get as a woman traveller? ▼
Look for a policy that covers: medical evacuation (minimum $1M), trip cancellation, lost luggage, and activity-specific cover if you plan to hike or dive. Brands recommended by our community include SafetyWing (great for long-term nomads), World Nomads, and Allianz. Always read the fine print — especially around "dangerous activities" clauses if you're adventurous.
How do I deal with unwanted attention while travelling? ▼
This is something most women encounter at some point — you're not alone, and it's never your fault. Strategies our community swears by: walking with purpose and confidence, wearing a fake wedding ring in certain countries, learning to say a firm "no" in the local language, heading into a shop or café if followed, and booking accommodations with a 24-hour front desk. Dressing to local customs also helps, not because you owe it to anyone, but because it tends to reduce unwanted attention in conservative regions.
Can I write for SweetTravel? ▼
Absolutely — and we'd love to hear from you! SweetTravel features real stories from real women in our community. All contributors are verified community members. If you've had a travel experience that would genuinely help other women, send us a pitch via the Contact page. We especially love stories from underrepresented destinations and experiences.
